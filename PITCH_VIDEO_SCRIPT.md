# ProcureIQ — Pitch Video Script
## 5-6 Minute Full Recording Guide

---

## LOOM SETUP (Do this first — 10 minutes)

1. Go to **loom.com** → sign up free with Google
2. Download the desktop app
3. Choose **Screen + Camera** mode (NOT screen only)
4. Camera bubble → Bottom Right, Size Small
5. Plug in earphones → select them as mic in Loom audio settings
6. Do a **30-second test recording** before the real take
7. Sit facing a window — light on your face, not behind you
8. Look at the **camera lens** (not your screen) on opening and closing lines

---

## PRE-RECORDING CHECKLIST

- [ ] Phone on silent, face-down
- [ ] Laptop notifications OFF (Focus Assist / Do Not Disturb)
- [ ] Earphones selected as mic in Loom
- [ ] Dashboard open at **http://localhost:3000** and ready
- [ ] Script on your phone beside you — NOT on the recording screen
- [ ] Room quiet, door closed
- [ ] Test recording done and watched back
- [ ] Script practiced **5 times out loud** (not in your head)

## FULL SCRIPT

> Lines starting with `//` are director notes — do NOT say them out loud.
> Everything else — speak exactly as written, then make it your own.

### ACT 1 — THE HOOK (0:00 – 0:10)
*// Look at camera. Speak slowly.*

"Every company buys things."

"Raw materials. Software. Services. Equipment. It never stops."

"But the way most companies make buying decisions?"

"It is completely broken."

---

### ACT 1 — THE PROBLEM (0:10 – 1:00)
*// Stay on dashboard — let them see the live system while you tell the story.*

"Meet a procurement manager at a large manufacturing company."

"Every morning she opens her laptop to dozens of supplier emails."

"Prices changing. Weather disrupting supply chains. Currency rates shifting overnight."

"Her job is to look at all of it — and decide: do we buy today, or do we wait?"

"Sounds simple. But here is the reality."

"She is manually checking weather websites. Currency apps. News articles in languages she cannot read."

"She is copying numbers into spreadsheets. Building her own formulas. Guessing."

"By the time she has a decision — the market has already moved."

"She is not making strategy. She is just surviving the data."

---

### ACT 1 — THE INDUSTRY SCALE (1:00 – 1:40)
*// Stay on dashboard.*

"And this is not one company's problem."

"CEAT Tyres alone spends Rs. 3,800 crore every year on natural rubber."

"One wrong call — buying too early, buying from the wrong supplier, missing a weather signal —"

"can cost crores in a single week."

"The tools have not kept up."

"Procurement managers are still using Excel, email, and gut feel"

"to make decisions worth hundreds of crores."

"There has to be a better way."

---

### ACT 2 — THE SOLUTION (1:40 – 2:10)
*// Look at camera when saying ProcureIQ.*

"That is why we built **ProcureIQ**."

"An AI-powered procurement intelligence system"

"that reads live data from around the world — every 30 minutes —"

"and tells the manager exactly what to do."

"Not a report. Not a dashboard full of numbers."

"A clear decision. BUY or WAIT. With the reasoning shown."

---

### ACT 2 — WHAT IT DOES (2:10 – 2:30)
*// Stay on dashboard overview.*

"ProcureIQ connects to real live data sources right now as I speak."

"Weather across four rubber-producing regions — Kerala, Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia."

"Bangkok Exchange rubber prices. Currency rates. RSS news feeds in five languages."

"And Harrisons Malayalam's private harvest schedule — internal data no competitor can see."

"All of it processed. All of it scored. All of it turned into one recommendation."

---

### ACT 3 — THE LIVE DEMO (2:30 – 5:00)

#### Step 1 — Show the Dashboard (2:30 – 2:50)
*// Point to the top of the dashboard.*

"This is the ProcureIQ dashboard."

"Clean. Simple. Built for a procurement manager — not a data scientist."

"Look at the top right — Live Data Connected. This is pulling real data right now."

"And here — Bangkok market opens in — a live countdown."

"Because timing matters. Buy before the market opens and you lock in today's price."

---

#### Step 2 — Run the Analysis (2:50 – 3:20)
*// Click the Refresh Analysis button slowly.*

"I click **Refresh Analysis**."

"Watch what happens."

*// Let the pipeline log appear line by line.*

"The system is now checking weather across four countries."

"Translating news articles from Thai, Vietnamese, Bahasa Indonesia, Hindi — into English."

"Running them through Gemini AI to extract supply signals."

"Cross-checking against two years of supplier history."

"Scoring every supplier. Checking confidence."

"Seven layers of AI — in under ten seconds."

---

#### Step 3 — Show the Recommendation (3:20 – 4:00)
*// Point to the BUY card in the center.*

"And here is the result."

"**BUY. Harrisons Malayalam.**"

"Confidence: 85%. Above the 55% threshold — so the system recommends."

"Why Harrisons? Look at the supplier ranking."

*// Point to the table.*

"Quality score 92. Lead time 95 — they can deliver in 5 days."

"The formula is right here — Quality 40%, Price 30%, Delivery Speed 20%, Green Score 10%."

"Harrisons scores 85 out of 100. Thai Rubber Co scores 78. Vietnamese Rubber Group scores 77."

"The system picked Harrisons because their harvest is ready, Kerala weather is optimal,"

"and their 5-day delivery beats everyone else by a week."

---

#### Step 4 — Show the Signal Feed (4:00 – 4:20)
*// Point to the right column.*

"And look at the signals that drove this decision."

"Live news from Thailand — in Thai — with English translation side by side."

"Vietnam supply update. Indonesian export data. Reuters commodities."

"And this one — marked with a lock — Harrisons Malayalam internal harvest data."

"Private. Encrypted. No competitor knows their harvest schedule."

"That is the edge."

---

#### Step 5 — Show the What If Simulator (4:20 – 4:50)
*// Scroll down to the purple What If section.*

"Now — the most important thing I want to show you."

"Judges always ask: is this hardcoded? Does it always say Harrisons?"

"Watch."

*// Click Kerala Floods button.*

"I change one condition. Kerala is flooding. Harvest delayed two weeks."

"Look at the ranking."

*// Point to the result.*

"Harrisons drops to number 4. Thai Rubber Co moves to number 1."

"The recommendation changes automatically."

*// Click Harvest Not Available.*

"Harrisons stock not ready — Thai Rubber Co wins again."

*// Click Mixed Signals.*

"Signals are contradictory — confidence drops to 43%."

"Below 55% — the system says **WAIT**. No order. Silence is smarter than a wrong Rs. 10 crore order."

"The system is not biased toward any supplier."

"It recommends whoever is best for today's actual conditions."

---

#### Step 6 — Place the Order (4:50 – 5:00)
*// Click Place Order.*

"When the manager agrees — she clicks Place Order."

"The full purchase order is auto-drafted. Vendor, quantity, price basis, delivery date."

"She reviews it. Clicks Confirm. It goes to SAP."

"The entire process — from live data to approved order — in under two minutes."

---

### ACT 3 — THE TRANSFORMATION (5:00 – 5:30)
*// Slow down here. Speak with weight.*

"Before ProcureIQ — hours of manual checking. Spreadsheets. Guesswork. Missed signals."

"After ProcureIQ — live data. AI analysis. Clear decision. Order placed."

"The manager is not a data processor anymore."

"She is a strategic decision-maker."

"That is the transformation ProcureIQ delivers."

"Not just for one company. For every procurement team making high-stakes buying decisions."

---

### CLOSING (5:30 – 6:00)
*// Look directly at the camera. Speak slowly and with confidence.*

"The future of procurement is not more spreadsheets."

"It is not more emails. It is not more manual work."

"The future of procurement is intelligent."

"We built ProcureIQ to make that future available today."

"Thank you."

## VOICE DELIVERY TIPS

- **Speak 20% slower** than feels natural — nerves make you rush
- **Pause 1 full second** after "surviving the data" — let it land
- **Say ProcureIQ with energy** at 1:40 — it is the answer to everything
- **Stress TEN SECONDS** and **FIVE SECONDS** — slow down on these words
- **Look at the camera lens** on the opening line and every closing line
- **Record 3 full takes minimum** — take 1 is nervous, take 3 is confident
- **Smile on the closing line** — confidence is contagious through a screen
